Description:

Montelukast is a leukotriene receptor antagonist used in asthma therapy, exercise-induced breathing issues, and allergies.

Chemical Information:

  • Formula: C35H36ClNO3S
  • Category: Leukotriene Receptor Antagonist (LRA)

Dosage Forms:

  • Tablets, chewable tablets, granules
  • Strengths: 4mg, 5mg, 10mg

Indications:

  • Asthma: ≥12 months
  • Exercise-Induced Bronchoconstriction: ≥6 years
  • Allergic Rhinitis: Seasonal (≥2 years), Perennial (≥6 months)

Mechanism of Action:

Blocks leukotriene receptor CysLT1 to reduce inflammation, mucus, and bronchoconstriction.

Pharmacokinetics:

  • Absorption: 64% bioavailability
  • Metabolism: CYP2C8, CYP3A4, CYP2C9
  • Excretion: Bile/feces
  • Half-life: 2.7–5.5 hours

Adverse Effects:

  • Fatigue, behavior changes, cramps, rash, etc.

Contraindications:

  • Neuropsychiatric disorders, aspirin sensitivity, hepatic issues

Interactions:

  • Food: No effect
  • Drugs: Acetaminophen, fluconazole
  • Disease: Liver, neuro, pulmonary

Pregnancy & Lactation:

  • Category B – Use only if clearly needed
  • Breastfeeding: Caution advised

Off-Label Uses:

  • Chronic urticaria, eczema, AERD, cognitive disorders
